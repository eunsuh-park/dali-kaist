Research

1. Situational Theory of Problem Solving (STOPS)

  • Understanding how and why publics engage in communicative actions
  • Examining proactiveness and reactiveness in information acquisition, selection, and transmission
  • Developing the Communicative Action in Problem Solving (CAPS) framework

2. Cognitive Biases and Epistemic Inertia

  • Investigating cognitive arrest among lay problem solvers
  • Studying epistemic inertia in public decision-making
  • Developing debiasing interventions

3. Information Markets and Pseudo-Information

  • Analyzing information trafficking among social actors
  • Detecting and mitigating opinion spam in digital spaces
  • Understanding the dynamics of failing information markets

4. Strategic Communication

  • Crisis communication
  • Health communication
  • Government communication and public diplomacy
